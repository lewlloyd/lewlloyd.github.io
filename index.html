Hey! I'm Lewis.

I'm a freelance researcher working at the intersection of politics, technology, climate change and governance.

I'm interested in what the future looks like, with a particular focus on whether 'better' futures are possible and how we might realise them. This relates not only to the sorts of 'policy' changes that might be required, but also to ways of changing culture and behaviour more broadly, improving governance structures and decision-making, and updating the infrastructure available to support interesting projects, initiatives and communities.

I'm currently a researcher with the School of International Futures and am supporting the Infected Blood Inquiry's expert group on Public Health and Administration.

Prior to going freelance, I spect 3 years at the Institute for Government working on Brexit, ways to improve policy making, and the UK government's use of data and technology. There's a full list here of the reports and articles I had published while I was there.

My research has been featured on the BBC and in the FT, The Guardian, The Times and elsewhere, and I've provided commentary on television, radio and podcasts, including Al Jazeera, BBC Radio and LBC.

To chat about a research, policy or other project, or just to say hi, drop me a line: lewisalexanderlloyd@gmail.com.
