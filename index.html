Hi! I'm Lewis.

I'm a freelance researcher and writer working at the intersection of politics, technology, climate change and governance.

I'm interested in what the future looks like, with a particular focus on whether 'better' futures are possible and how we might realise them.

Currently, I work as a researcher at the School of International Futures and am supporting the Infected Blood Inquiry's expert group on Public Health and Administration. Prior to going freelance, I spect 3 years at the Institute for Government working on Brexit, ways to improve policy making, and the UK government's use of data and technology.

My research has been featured on the BBC and in the FT, The Guardian, The Times and elsewhere, and I have provided commentary on television, radio and podcasts, including Al Jazeera, BBC Radio and LBC.

For reports and articles I've had published, there's a list here.

For what I'm thinking about at the moment, try the blog or Twitter.

To chat about a research, policy or other project, or just to say hi, drop me a line: lewisalexanderlloyd@gmail.com.
