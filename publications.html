Reports for the IfG ( * indicates lead authorship):

* Policy Making in a Digital World (October 2020)
Solo project looking at how government could make better use of data and digital technology to improve the way it makes policy. Based on a year of research, including interviews with a variety of policy makers and technologists inside and outside government, and covering issues such as knowledge management, modelling and forecasting, and online public deliberation. Lots of the more interesting content (at least to my mind) didn't make the final report, so I've written some blog posts fleshing out the published material here. Made it into Politico London Playbook and was picked up by UKAuthority and ZDNet.

Becoming a Junior Minister (December 2019)
A report pulling together lessons from the IfG's Ministers Reflect interviews. Main personal takeaway from the project is that our political system, where we regularly drop people with little to no knowledge of an area into a position where they are in charge of it for the country as a whole, offering them hardly any time to get up to speed and moving them on to another area within a couple of years, is completely bonkers.

Negotiating Brexit: Preparing for talks on the UK's future relationship with the EU (April 2019)
Assessing what the UK government got wrong (and, in a handful of cases, right) in the first phase of the Brexit negotiations and setting out how it should approach the more complex negotiations on the nature of the future UK-EU relationship.

* The Brexit Effect (March 2019)
Pulling together research and analysis from across the IfG looking at the impact of Brexit on different aspects of government, including the civil service, parliament, and devolution. Published on the day the UK was originally supposed to leave the EU: 29 March 2019.

Brexit: six months to go (September 2018)
A summary and assessment of the progress made by government in preparing for Brexit, with only 6 months to go before the originally planned exit date (29 March 2019). Referenced in the Guardian and...

Negotiating Brexit: policing and criminal justice (September 2018)
An overview of UK-EU cooperation on policing and criminal justice (covering the European Arrest Warrant, Europol, the SIS II database and more) and how it might be affected by Brexit. Particularly focused on what would happen if there was 'no deal' on future cooperation, and how a mutually acceptable agreement between the UK and EU might be reached. One of the most interesting projects I've worked on. Picked up in the Independent, New Statesman...

Preparing Brexit: How ready is Whitehall? (June 2018)
A detailed report based on lots of interviews with senior civil servants looking at the main obstacles preventing the UK government from preparing for Brexit effectively, and how they might be overcome. Picked up in the Observer, The Times the Express, the Wall Street Journal and others.

Negotiating Brexit: the views of the EU27 (March 2018)
Setting out the different interests and views of the EU member states for the UK to bear in mind in the Brexit negotiations.

Costing Brexit: what is Whitehall spending on exiting the EU? (March 2018)
Looking at the spending of six key government departments on Brexit. The research underpinned this BBC Reality Check piece and was reported on the BBC 10 o'clock news. Also picked up by CNN, The Scotsman and others.

Who's Afraid of the ECJ?: Charting the UK's relationship with the European Court (December 2017)
A quantitative analysis of the UK's relationship with the ECJ on a range of issues. I led on the data collection, analysis and visualisation. Findings referred to in the FT, the Guardian and elsewhere.
